&lt;p style=" margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px; -qt-block-indent:0; text-indent:0px;">&lt;span style=" font-family:'.AppleSystemUIFont'; font-size:13pt;">Raspberry Pi Pico and Pico 2. Based around the RP2040 and RP2350 microcontrollers respectively, Picos are low cost microcontroller device bringing Raspberry Pi quality, cost, and easy of use to the microcontroller space. Jan 2021, Split part in to two versions: tht (this one) and SMD. This one will drill holes for a .1 in header in pcb to allow plugging in the module as well as providing pads to solder the module to the top of the board if desired. The SMD varient is single sided and uses the castellation pads to solder the module to the board (no holes!). Added mounting holes and board outline to silkscreen. To drill mounting holes the user needs to drag a hole from core parts/pcb over the hole in silkscreen in pcb view. By default the mounting holes will not be drilled. The areas marked KEEPOUT in pcb silkscreen should not have traces running through them to avoid shorts on a soldered in module!&lt;/span>&lt;/p>&lt;/body>&lt;/html></description>
